Marcus Aurelius

The Philosopher Emperor who guided Rome through crisis

Here's a time line of Marcus Aurelius's life:

  • 121 CE - Born in Rome to a prominent political family
  • 127 CE - Catches the attention of Emperor Hadrian, who nicknames him "Verissimus" (most truthful) for his honest character
  • 132 CE - Begins studying philosophy under Diognetus, who introduces him to Stoic philosophy and ethical living
  • 136 CE - Betrothed to Ceionia Fabia at Hadrian's arrangement, though this would later be changed
  • 138 CE - Adopted by Antoninus Pius as part of the succession plan arranged by Hadrian, making him heir to the empire
  • 145 CE - Marries Faustina the Younger, daughter of Antoninus Pius. Their marriage would last 31 years and produce 13 children
  • 161 CE - Becomes Emperor upon the death of Antoninus Pius, immediately making his adopted brother Lucius Verus co-emperor
  • 165 CE - Faces the first major crisis of his reign as the Antonine Plague begins spreading throughout the empire
  • 167 CE - Personally leads troops against Germanic tribes in the Marcomannic Wars while continuing to write philosophical reflections
  • 169 CE - Death of co-emperor Lucius Verus forces Marcus to bear sole responsibility for the empire during increasingly difficult times
  • 170-174 CE - Writes much of his philosophical work "Meditations" during military campaigns, never intending it for publication
  • 175 CE - Deals with the rebellion of Avidius Cassius in Syria, showing clemency to the rebel's family after his defeat
  • 176 CE - Establishes four chairs of philosophy in Athens, one for each major school: Platonic, Aristotelian, Stoic, and Epicurean
  • 178 CE - Returns to the northern frontier with his son Commodus to continue the Germanic campaigns
  • 180 CE - Dies at Vindobona (modern Vienna) during a military campaign, leaving the empire to his son Commodus

"Accept the things to which fate binds you, and love the people with whom fate brings you together, but do so with all your heart."

-- Marcus Aurelius, Meditations
